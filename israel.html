Aerial view of Masada’s mountaintop ruins. Herod the Great developed Masada as a palace-fortress beginning around 35 BCE. It later became the final Jewish rebel stronghold during the First Jewish–Roman War, falling to the Romans in 73 or 74 CE.

Entrance sign at Masada National Park.

Topographical model of Mount Masada displayed inside the Masada Visitor Center.
